# 5. Conclusion

In this paper, we present a unified dual-mask physical model for light field depth estimation, addressing the inherent failure of Epipolar Plane Image (EPI) linear assumptions on non-Lambertian surfaces. By analyzing the physical and geometric properties of light ray interactions, we establish a robust framework that explicitly isolates regions where conventional reflectance assumptions break down. The proposed approach integrates a three-layer angular signal decomposition with a geometry-based material classification mechanism, enabling reliable routing for depth estimation in complex mixed scenes.

Specifically, the main contributions of this work are summarized as follows:

*   **Three-layer angular signal decomposition and geometry-based material classification:** We reveal the physical mechanisms by which non-Lambertian surfaces disrupt EPI linearity. By leveraging geometric features instead of frequency-domain representations, the proposed mechanism overcomes the resolution limitations of discrete light field sampling (e.g., $9 \times 9$). This provides a reliable theoretical basis for material-aware routing, preventing erroneous depth computations in physically invalid regions.
*   **Dual-mask routing architecture:** We design a dual-mask mechanism that structurally isolates regions with conflicting physical assumptions. This architectural separation effectively mitigates the interference between Lambertian and non-Lambertian signals, allowing the network to process complex urban and mixed scenes with enhanced precision and stability.
*   **Domain-balanced mixed training strategy:** To address the severe scarcity of non-Lambertian training data and domain shift issues, we introduce a domain-balanced sampling and mixed training protocol. This strategy maximizes the utilization of available synthetic data, significantly improving the overall depth estimation accuracy (achieving an overall MAE of 0.133 and a mixed urban MAE of 0.081) and demonstrating the feasibility of enhancing generalization through architectural and training optimizations under data-constrained conditions.

We hope that the proposed unified dual-mask physical model will serve as a strong baseline for future light field 3D perception tasks and encourage further research on physics-aware architectures for complex visual environments. The integration of physical reflectance models with deep learning routing mechanisms provides a promising direction for advancing robust depth estimation in real-world autonomous navigation and immersive video systems.
