# 5. Conclusion

In this paper, we propose a unified dual-mask physical model and a domain-balanced training strategy for light field depth estimation, addressing the inherent photo-consistency violations in non-Lambertian and mixed-reflectance scenes. By integrating multi-directional epipolar plane images (EPIs) with a lightweight baseline architecture, our approach effectively disentangles complex view-dependent reflections from geometric structures, establishing a robust framework for diverse real-world environments.

The core contributions of this work are summarized as follows:
* **Unified Architecture and Domain-Balanced Training:** We constructed a lightweight, efficient baseline model capable of simultaneously processing scenes with diverse reflectance properties. By leveraging multi-directional EPIs and a domain-balanced training strategy, the proposed model significantly improves depth estimation accuracy across both overall and domain-specific metrics (e.g., achieving an Overall MAE of 0.1795 and a Mixed/Urban MAE of 0.1687), providing a strong and practical baseline for non-Lambertian light field depth estimation.
* **Falsification of Frequency-Domain Material Classification at Low Angular Resolutions:** We rigorously falsified the prevailing assumption that a $9 \times 9$ angular resolution supports frequency-domain material classification. Our analysis reveals that the non-DC spectral energy is less than 3%, yielding only approximately four effective non-DC frequency points—far below the $>10$ points required for BRDF characterization. This negative result establishes a clear hardware and design boundary, indicating that future frequency-based light field analyses require at least a $17 \times 17$ angular sampling resolution to avoid futile efforts.
* **Identification of the EPI Architectural Ceiling in Complex Lambertian Scenes:** We explicitly defined the performance ceiling of traditional EPI-based methods in complex Lambertian scenarios (MAE $\approx$ 0.20), demonstrating that this physical and architectural limit cannot be breached merely by increasing resolution or refining data. This finding redirects the evolutionary path of light field depth estimation, substantiating the necessity of incorporating non-EPI modalities or hybrid routing architectures to overcome inherent structural bottlenecks.

Despite the demonstrated efficacy of our unified model in mixed and non-Lambertian domains, an honest limitation remains in its pure EPI-centric design, which inherently caps the performance in complex Lambertian regions (resulting in a Lambertian MAE of 0.2443) due to the identified architectural ceiling. To transcend this fundamental boundary, our specific future direction is to develop a hybrid routing architecture that dynamically integrates defocus cues and explicit correspondence matching with EPI features, thereby bypassing the linear-structure constraints of traditional epipolar geometry.
